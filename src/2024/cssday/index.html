CSS Day

@CSSDayConf 2024

Event
2024-06-06T06:44:00Z

I’ve once again visited Amsterdam, attending the CSS Day conference for the third time. They have their 10th anniversary this year. 🎉

In case you are wondering, a CSS Day cannot be squeezed into a single day. Contrary to my previous visits in 2016 and 2018, where Thursday had a special theme with dedicated talks, this time it was just a plethora of great topics scattered across both days.

Day 1

And this is the first half of the plethora.

Talks & Speakers

  • “Web Design Engineering With The New CSS” by Matthias Ott
  • “CSS Layout” by Rachel Andrew
  • “Utility-First CSS” by Sarah Dayan
  • “Character Modeling in CSS” by Julia Miocene
  • “Problems Solved by OpenType Details subjectively improved by OpenType Layout Features and CSS” by Roel Nieskens
  • “Standardization Stories” by Elika Etemad
  • “The Garden and The Treadmill” by Stephen Hay

Day 2

As if the first day wasn’t amazing already, look at what Friday had to offer.

Talks & Speakers

  • “Over-engineering Your CSS” by Kevin Powell
  • “Accessibility and CSS” by Sara Soueidan
  • “Anchor Positioning” by Tab Atkins-Bittner
  • “Scroll-enhanced Experiences” by Carmen Ansio
  • “Impactful Experimentation” by Roma Komarov
  • “How to Teach CSS” by Josh Comeau
  • “CSS Masonry Layout” by Nicole Sullivan
