Frontend Graz meetup #10

Formerly known as CSS-in-Graz.

Event
2026-06-29T20:50:00Z

I’ve traveled to Graz to for once *not* speak at the Frontend Graz meetup, and instead be a regular attendee who can simply enjoy the show. By the way, this was the tenth edition, because we include the times when it was still called CSS-in-Graz.

Train delays and extreme heat cannot take away from the fact that it was once again a great experience.

Talks & Speakers

  • “Fighting Hardware Obsolescence With Greener Frontend Code” by Björn Ganslandt
  • “Rslidy: Lightweight, Accessible, and Responsive HTML5 Slide Decks” by Keith Andrews
  • “Web Performance: What Actually Matters - a deep dive into Core Web Vitals, performance debugging, and real-world optimizations” by Stefan Schintler

(Short) Summary

Björn kicked it off with a topic that truly hit home, fighting hardware obsolescence is something I deeply care about. Progressive enhancement and testing on older devices can keep hardware around for longer, thus slowing down the increase of e-waste.

Keith then talked about Rslidy, which allows you to run HTML slide decks in the browser. Its file size is already impressive, but guess what, it comes with zero dependencies. Goes without saying, Rslidy has been used to present Rslidy, clearly a case of dogfooding.

Finally, Stefan sealed the deal by talking about what actually matters when it comes to web performance, with his fresh take on Lighthouse scores.
