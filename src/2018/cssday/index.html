CSS Day

@CSSDayConf 2018

Event
2018-06-14T06:36:00Z

I was in Amsterdam, attending the CSS Day conference for the second time.

Day 1

While the actual CSS Day took place on Friday, they once again added a special on Thursday. When I was there two years ago it was about HTML, the year before I missed out on Browser APIs, but in 2018 all eyes were on User Experience (UX).

Talks & Speakers

  • “From Purpose to Patterns” by Alla Kholmatova
  • “Build Bridges, not Walls” by Jenny Shen
  • “Ten Ways to Irritate Your Design Team” by Jane Austin
  • “Motion & Playfulness” by Benjamin De Cock
  • “A Year of Exotic Data Visualisation” by Nadieh Bremer
  • “From the Dark Corners of eCommerce” by Vitaly Friedman
  • “User Testing on Any Budget” by Ida Aalen
  • “The Accidental Leader” by Andy Budd

Day 2

Why wouldn’t you want to learn about CSS in Amsterdam? As if the UX Special the day before wasn’t already crazy good, the line-up for the actual CSS Day has been phenomenal. And by the end of the day I’ve learned a lot.

Talks & Speakers

  • “The Friction of Web Standards” by Eric Meyer
  • “Web Typography” by Richard Rutter
  • “Great Graphic Design” by Hidde de Vries
  • “SVG Filters - The Crash Course” by Sara Soueidan
  • “Solving Container Queries Today” by Greg Whitworth
  • “Box Alignment” by Hui Jing Chen
  • “CSS for the Next Billion Users” by Ire Aderinokun
  • “CSS-in-JS: FTW || WTF?” by Bruce Lawson
